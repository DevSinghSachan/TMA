Professor Hanna Wallach (Computer Science, UMass Amherst) is seeking a
postdoctoral researcher for an new exciting project at the
intersection of machine learning and computational social science. The
project involves developing new statistical methods for characterizing
and predicting scientific and technological emergence.
The ideal candidate must be proficient in a subset of the following:
* Bayesian statistics
* topic modeling
* social network analysis
* analyzing massive text corpora
* scientometics
* information visualization
He or she will be an active participant in Hanna Wallach's research
group, collaborating not only with her and her graduate students, but
also Andrew McCallum, David Jensen, Ben Marlin, and other faculty.
This is a one year (renewable) position.
A Ph.D. in Computer Science, Statistics, or related fields is required.
Applications should include a cover letter, a CV, one or two
representative publications, and two letters of recommendation. To
apply, please email application materials to wallach at cs.umass.edu.
UMass Amherst is an Affirmative Action/Equal Opportunity Employer.
Women and members of minority groups are encouraged to apply.
