Variation in the length of documents should not matter in terms of finding
good topics. In terms of getting good estimates of the posterior
distribution of topics for a given document, shorter documents might have
more variability in their estimated distributions, but should still work.
Welling, Teh, and Kappen (UAI 2008) has a discussion of the effect of
small counts on various inference algorithms:
http://www.ics.uci.edu/~welling/publications/papers/HybridVBMC-v6.pdf
